Malaria: A leading cause of morbidity and mortality among children younger than 5 years.
Infant Mortality: In the 1980s, Benin's infant mortality rate was 203 deaths per 1,000 live births.
Maternal Health: As of 2015, Benin had the 26th highest rate of maternal mortality in the world.
Female Genital Mutilation: According to a 2013 UNICEF report, 13% of women had undergone female genital mutilation.
Child Health Care Access: Initially, only 1 in 3 mothers had access to child health care services. The Bamako Initiative improved access and efficiency of healthcare services.
Health Care Reform: Community-based healthcare reform introduced through the Bamako Initiative improved the provision of health services.
Healthcare Surveys: The Demographic and Health Surveys have been tracking health issues in Benin since 1996.
Public Health Investment: In 2013, the government dedicated 2.5% of GDP to public health.
