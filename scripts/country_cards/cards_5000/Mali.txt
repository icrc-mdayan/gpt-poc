Health Information in Mali
Health Challenges:
High levels of poverty, malnutrition, and inadequate hygiene and sanitation.
Life expectancy at birth was estimated to be 53.06 years in 2012.
In 2000, 62-65% of the population had access to safe drinking water, and 69% had access to sanitation services.
Healthcare Spending:
In 2001, government expenditures on health were about US$4 per capita.
Nutrition Improvement:
Efforts to improve nutrition include projects like ICRISAT and the Aga Khan Foundation training women to make nutritious local recipes (e.g., equinut).
Medical Facilities:
Medical facilities are very limited, and medicines are often in short supply.
Prevalent Diseases:
Malaria and other arthropod-borne diseases.
Infectious diseases such as cholera and tuberculosis.
High rate of child malnutrition and low immunization rates.
HIV/AIDS:
Estimated 1.9% of the adult and children population was afflicted with HIV/AIDS, among the lowest rates in Sub-Saharan Africa.
Female Genital Mutilation (FGM):
An estimated 85-91% of Mali's girls and women have undergone FGM (2006 and 2001 data).
Mali faces numerous health challenges, particularly due to preventable diseases and inadequate health infrastructure. Despite efforts to improve nutrition and health conditions, the country continues to struggle with high rates of child malnutrition, low immunization, and limited access to medical facilities and safe drinking water.
